#Header: WHAT IS A BUNNY
#Content: Bunnies are small, furry mammals that belong to the rabbit family. They have long ears, a short tail, and a soft, fluffy coat. Bunnies are herbivores and their diet consists mainly of hay, vegetables, and fruits.

#Slide: 3
#Header: BUNNY CARE
#Content: Bunnies need a spacious cage or hutch that is at least 3 feet by 3 feet. The cage should have a solid bottom and a wire top. Bunnies also need a litter box, a food bowl, and a water bottle.

#Slide: 4
#Header: BUNNY HEALTH
#Content: Bunnies are generally healthy animals, but they can be susceptible to certain health problems, such as dental disease, ear infections, and respiratory infections. It is important to take your bunny to the vet for regular checkups.

#Slide: 5
#Header: BUNNY BREEDS
#Content: There are many different breeds of bunnies, each with its own unique characteristics. Some of the most popular breeds include the Dutch, the Flemish Giant, the Lop, and the Rex.

#Slide: 6
#Header: BUNNY FUN FACTS
#Content: Bunnies can jump up to 3 feet high.
Bunnies can run up to 7 miles per hour.
Bunnies have a lifespan of 8 to 12 years.
Bunnies are social animals and live in groups called colonies.
